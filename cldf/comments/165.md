# Kurdish affixes in Suleimaniya Jewish Neo-Aramaic


Examples and information are from [Khan ](sources.bib?label=Khan+#cldf:khan2007a)([2007](sources.bib?label=2007#cldf:khan2007a)). All three forms are described as being not very tightly integrated into the morphology, as they only occur in word-final positions. They are, however, presented as bound forms.


1 definite suffix

- _-ăke_ ‘definite’, e.g. _barux-awal-ăke_ (friend-plural-definite) ‘the friends’. The source form of this suffix is a Kurdish particle _aka_ with a Kurdish oblique case marker _-y_.



1 post-verbal particle

- -_(a)wa_ ‘returning, restoring, repetition, completion’, e.g. _hiye-wa_ (come:past:3.masculine-particle) ‘he came back’, _qadome der-awa_ (tomorrow/return:fut:3masculine-particle) ‘he will return tomorrow.’



1 nominal modifying particle

- _-iš_ ‘inclusive’, e.g. _nošew-iš_ ‘also himself’.
