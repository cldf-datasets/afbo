# Arabic affixes in Indonesian, standard


Information and examples are from [Jones ](sources.bib?label=Jones+#cldf:jones1984a)([1984](sources.bib?label=1984#cldf:jones1984a)), [Verhaar ](sources.bib?label=Verhaar+#cldf:verhaar1984a)([1984](sources.bib?label=1984#cldf:verhaar1984a)), [Tadmor ](sources.bib?label=Tadmor+#cldf:tadmor2009a)([2009](sources.bib?label=2009#cldf:tadmor2009a)) and personal communications from Uri Tadmor. [Jones ](sources.bib?label=Jones+#cldf:jones1984a)([1984](sources.bib?label=1984#cldf:jones1984a):12–17) discusses loanwords and some “Arabic phrases and bound morphemes; Arabic grammar”, but it is unclear whether these are used with native Indonesian stems. According to Uri Tadmor (personal communication), only one affix from Arabic is attested with non-Arabic stems.


1 suffix

- _-awi_ ‘adjectivizer’ ([Tadmor 2009](sources.bib?label=Tadmor+2009#cldf:tadmor2009a):705), e.g. _gerejawi_ ‘related to church’


Another Arabic adjectivizer, _-iah_, occurs only on Arabic roots and on roots perceived as Arabic according to Uri Tadmor (personal communication).
