# Ancient Greek affixes in Latin


Information from [Magni ](sources.bib?label=Magni+#cldf:magni2017a)([2017](sources.bib?label=2017#cldf:magni2017a)), who provides detailed argumentation for the borrowing process of this affix, and cites the examples given here from [Adams ](sources.bib?label=Adams+#cldf:adams2013a)([2013](sources.bib?label=2013#cldf:adams2013a):564–565). There may be other affixes borrowed from Ancient Greek into Latin, but [Magni ](sources.bib?label=Magni+#cldf:magni2017a)([2017](sources.bib?label=2017#cldf:magni2017a)) focuses on this one only.


1 adjectivizer

- _-ĭnus_ _‘__adjectivizer__’, e.g._ _mĕl-ĭnus_ ‘made of honey’_,_ _cupressĭnus_ ‘made of cypress’, _fungĭnus_ ‘made of mushroom’, _laurĭnus_ ‘made of laurel’, _nucĭnus_ ‘made of nut’, _triticĭnus_ ‘made of wheat’
